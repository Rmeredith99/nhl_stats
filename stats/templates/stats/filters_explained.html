Why use a filter?

Filters are useful for eliminating data that you don't want. By filtering out a subset of the data, you can easily see just what you're interested in.


How do you write a filter?

Filters are easy to write! Simply start with the name of the statistic which you want filtered, add a comparison operator (<, <=, >, >=, =), and then add the value.

Capitalization and spacing are not important when writing a filter; "gamesPlayed", "Games Played", and "Ga MeSpL aYeD" are all equivalent.

You can also string together multiple filters. Simply start writing your next filter on a new line.

If you're unsure of the type of value to filter by, check the statistic to see what sort of data it contains before filtering.


Examples
  • goals > 10
  • Players with more than 10 goals
  • gamesPlayed = 82
  • Players with exactly 82 games played
  • blockedShots <= 50
  • Players with no more than 50 blocked shots
  • playerTeamsPlayedFor = Buf
  • Players who played for Buffalo, even if briefly
    Note: playerTeamsPlayedFor is unique in that the comparison doesn't matter. It behaves the same for all operators.)
